The primer

Introduction

This document is a brief introduction to Evolution, a corporation in Eve Online. The information below will provide an outline of how Evolution functions and explain the basic guidelines of the corp.

Evol 101

Evol is team.
Evol is not a democracy. Molle runs Evolution.

Leaders

There are various directors, more then I shall list here. You will get to know them all in due time. What matters most is that the directors are just people like you. While they have added responsibilities, please keep in mind that they are also playing Eve to enjoy themselves.

Corp Activities

A common misconception is that Evolution is purely a PvP corporation. This is not true, we also have a very large and well organised industrial base. Our industrial team and logistics division put in a LOT of time, so that we all have the ships we need for PvP. Please lend them a hand when asked.

Everyone we accept has something they can offer the corp. Inventor? Explorer? PI? Making videos? All of these things bring value and are useful. We want people who are more than just a member in fleet hitting F1.

When you are accepted into Evolution, we accept YOU in. Not your character, not your toon, you.

New recruits

We do not typically openly recruit. Most of our members are referred to us by current members. In Evolution, new members are affectionately referred to as "biatches". EachAs a biatch, you will have a has a "Daddy,” who will be responsible for you. If you need anything or have any questions, you need to let them know.

Assets

As a biatch, you are expected to take care of your own in-game finances. That said, Evolution is part of an alliance and we may have specific doctrine ships that you will be asked to fly. Evolution will lend you non-capital, doctrine ships for usage. Capital ships will not typically be provided, unless a director deems it necessary.

Your Daddy will explain doctrines and how ships are checked in and out of corp hangars on IRC. Doctrine ships are not to be retained in personal hangars, neither by members or biatches.

When you become a full member, you are expected to divest your assets to Evolution. This would include isk, blueprints, ships etc. We are not looking to make you spacepoor or to destroy your game experience. When the time comes for you to be made a full member, your Daddy and a director will sit down with you and go over your assets together, and make a sensible decision. You must be comfortable with the fact that it is expected of everyone to contribute for the overall good of the corp, so we can keep those hangars full of the equipment our members need.

Members

Hold very little personal wealth and assets. The exceptions are some market traders or industry toons that are working towards corp goals and/or running operations pre-approved by Molle. It is ok to have interests outside of the corporation, as long as they do not conflict with our goals and Evolution is benefiting from the operation.

You should keep enough isk to cover basics like your clone in emergencies, basic needs etc. As a member, the corp wallet is available for you to use if you need to buy non-capital skill books. If you need capital skills, speak with a director and they will transfer the isk needed to cover them. If the wallet is getting low, please poke a director. Outside of basic needs, we do ask members to drop isk and other resources to the corp.

Evolution isn’t into strict accounting and itemized earning reports. We expect members to keep what they need and give the rest back to the corp. Evolution provides for you. If the corp needs something, we expect members to look through their assets and help provide for the corp.

The more willingness you show to help the corp, the more room for independent action you are given. It isn’t favoritism, it is giving people who have proven themselves over time, with words and actions, some additional latitude. This latitude will evolve over the time you spend in Evolution, and it is earned not given.

Plex

We know some of you need to pay for your accounts via plex. If you have an out of corp character to do industry, incursions or missions, to generate isk for plex, that’s okay. Having you logged in and playing with us, is important to us. However, please be reasonable and understand if it seems you are more committed to your outside interests than to Evolution, you will be asked to leave.

Hangars

Evolution will supply:

  • Doctrine ships in staging systems.
  • Some roaming options for people in staging system.
  • Doctrine capitals in staging system.
  • A few ships in carebear systems for members.
  • Ships for corp pve (mining) ops.
  • Mods, fuel, ammo for the above ships.
Evolution will not supply:
  • Incursion shiny (faction) ships.
  • T3/Faction ships for pve.
  • Personal Capital ships.
  • Plex.
  • High-end learning implants.

Evolution may be willing to build/sell you a capital or t2 ship at build cost, if production times, supplies and corp needs are permitting.

Corporation/Alliance Ops

As a part of Evolution, you are a part of the team. We can do nothing without you. While we understand there may be times you have to handle other matters, we do expect everyone to work together and play together. If you are not contributing to the corp or are into a loner gaming lifestyle, Evolution may not be a good fit for you.

Alliance PVP operations take precedence over all isk generation or other outside activities. If a CTA (mandatory op) is called, you are expected to place everything on hold and respond to the alliance’s call for pilots.

Insurance

Biatches - you insure your own personal ships. If you die, you keep your insurance to buy more ships/mods/whatever. (The only deviation from this policy will be above referenced doctrine ships). Your Daddy will explain the SRP.

Members - corp policy is 100% insurance coverage for subcaps. Ships should be insured from the corp hangar and using corp funds. We do not insure Capitals, unless it is deemed prudent by a director. Follow all SRP guidelines.

Forums

Information is routinely distributed to the corp on our forums. You should check these daily.

Teamspeak

Evolution uses teamspeak. You must use it. You simply can't pvp without it.

A few TS basics. Keep TS chatter to a minimum. In fleet operations, do not speak unless you have information you feel is vital to the fleet commanders' situational awareness. Do not talk about your night out, play music, your skill training plans or anything non-essential. It is a distraction to all and we do not tolerate it. YES, you may be in a channel where someone is doing some of the above things. Good for them. We DO NOT. There are Evolution-specific channels to chit chat in and get to know your corpmates. Use them. Often.

Important - From time to time people in other corps will join us on our TS server, or, we may have an opportunity to join them on theirs. DO NOT discuss corp information not related to the operation at hand. Do NOT discuss our office locations, blueprints, sales, plans, internal structure, anything. DO NOT go into other corp channels unless invited.

IRC/Jabber

IRC must be used. This is not optional. We seldom, if ever, use ingame chat. If you need help configuring IRC, speak to your Daddy or check the forums.

Jabber is used to notify you of ops and other important issues. If you need help setting this up please tell your Daddy.

SuperCaps

Supers and Titan class capital ships are hefty investments. Even the skills are hefty investments. As such, there are multiple requirements for them, that begin with, but are not limited to:

  • Be a full member.
  • Must have a dedicated alt character.
  • Be very active in game.
  • Advise Molle of your desire to fly one.
  • Additional information on Supers will be made available on our forums.

All in all, we work for you, you work for us.
